PERSONAL INFORMATION

The subject matter of data protection is your personal information. That is all the information that relates to you (as an identified or identifiable individual). This covers for example, information such as name, postal address, e-mail address, or telephone number as well as information that necessarily originates from the use of our Websites, such as details about the start, end, and scope of use, and the communication of your IP address.

TYPES OF PERSONAL INFORMATION

We collect and process various types of personal information, including your name, email address, password, username, billing address, and debit/credit card number. We do not process any sensitive personal information, such as health, biometric, or genetic data.

    • Otherwise contact us, or our registered partners in the course of our business.
  • Automated technology and data where you interact with our Websites and/or our Services, we automatically collect technical, usage, and marketing or communications data. We collect this personal information using particular service providers, cookies, and server logs/log files. This includes:
    • Browser and device information that is collected by most browsers or automatically through your device, such as your IP address, computer type, screen resolution, operating system name and version, device manufacturer and model, language, Internet browser type, and add-ons;
    • Log Information that is automatically collected in system logs about how you interact with our Websites, such as your online activity, search terms, pages you visit, date, and time of access. This information is used to improve your experience on our Websites and keep the Websites free of bugs and errors;
    • Cookies and similar technologies which serve us to communicate with your end device and exchange stored information. We will obtain your consent to our use of cookies where required by law.
  • Third parties or publicly available sources where your personal information is made available as follows:
    • Technical data from advertising networks, search information providers, and analytics providers (including Google Analytics);
    • Contact, financial, and transaction data from technical and payment services connected to our Services;
    • Identity and contact data made available from public sources, law enforcement, or government entities (as applicable);
    • Identity and contact data from third-party applications where services require the import of information about users and their clients to fulfill activities and facilitate payments.
INFORMATION PROCESSING

We process your personal information for the following purposes:

  • To provide and improve our Services, such as creating your account, delivering your courses, and providing customer support.
  • To communicate with you, such as sending you service updates, newsletters, and marketing offers, subject to your preferences and consent.
  • To ensure security and fraud prevention, such as verifying your identity, detecting and preventing unauthorized access, and protecting our rights and property.
  • To comply with the law, such as responding to legal requests, enforcing our terms and conditions, and protecting our legitimate interests.
HOW WE USE YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION, PURPOSES, AND LEGAL BASIS

We use your personal information as permitted by applicable law to operate, provide, develop and improve the Services. We are subject to the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR) when processing personal data of data subjects located in the European Union. Under the GDPR we are the data processor and data controller for your personal information when you access and use our Websites and Services. We process your personal information based on valid legal reasons, which may include your consent, our contractual obligations, our legal obligations, our vital interests, or our legitimate interests. We will inform you of the specific legal basis for each processing activity when we collect your personal information, or as soon as possible thereafter. You can withdraw your consent at any time, where applicable, by contacting us or using the opt-out links provided in our communications.

INFORMATION SHARING

We may share your personal information with third-party vendors, consultants, advertising and analytics partners, and service providers who help us provide, support and improve our services. These may include cloud storage providers, data analytics services, payment processors, sales and marketing tools, and artificial intelligence service providers. We only share your personal information with third parties who have agreed to process it in accordance with our instructions and this privacy notice, and who have implemented appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect it.

We may also share your personal information with public authorities, law enforcement agencies, courts, or other parties, if required or permitted by law, such as to comply with a legal obligation, to protect our rights or interests, or to defend ourselves from claims. We do not sell or rent your personal information to third parties for their own purposes, unless you have given us your explicit consent.

USE OF COOKIES AND TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES

We may use cookies and similar technologies to collect and store information about your use of our Services, such as your preferences, settings, and login status. Cookies are small files that are placed on your device when you visit our Websites. You can refuse or delete certain cookies through your browser settings, but some features of our Services may not function properly without them.

We may also use Google AdSense and other third-party advertising companies to display ads on our Websites, based on your browsing history and interests. These companies may use cookies and other tracking technologies to collect information about your visits to our Websites and other Websites, such as your IP address, device identifiers, and web pages viewed. You can opt out of the use of the DoubleClick DART cookie by visiting the Google ad and content network privacy policy. You can also opt out of the use of cookies by some third-party advertisers by visiting the Network Advertising Initiative opt-out page.

THIRD PARTY PLATFORM LOGINS

If you register or log in to our Services using your account details from a third party platform, such as Google or Apple, we may receive information from those providers, such as your name, email address, and profile picture. The information we receive depends on your privacy settings and the terms and policies of the third party platforms. We use this information to create and manage your account, and to personalize your experience on our Services. You can revoke our access to your third party platform accounts at any time by changing your settings on those platforms.

INFORMATION RETENTION

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which we collected it, or as required by law. We will delete or anonymize your personal information when it is no longer needed, or upon your request, unless we have a legal obligation or a legitimate interest to keep it. We will also erase your personal information if we are obliged to do so for other legal reasons. Applying these general principles, we will usually erase your personal information immediately after the legal permission has ceased to apply and provided that no other legal basis (e.g. commercial and tax law retention periods) intervenes. If the latter applies, we will delete the data after the other legal basis has ceased to apply. We will also usually erase your personal information if we are no longer required to hold it for the purposes we pursue and no other legal basis intervenes, if the latter is the case, we will delete the data after the other legal basis has ceased to apply.

INFORMATION SECURITY

We use organizational and technical security measures to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction. These measures include encryption, firewalls, access controls, and staff training. However, we cannot guarantee that your personal information is completely secure, as no method of transmission or storage over the internet is 100% secure. If you suspect any breach of your personal information, please contact us immediately.

INTERNATIONAL INFORMATION TRANSFERS

We may transfer your personal information to recipients in countries other than the country in which the information was originally collected. These countries may have different data protection laws than the country in which you provided the information.When we transfer your information to other countries, we will protect that information as described in this Privacy Policy and in accordance with applicable law. We store the information we collect in secure servers located in the United States. We rely on adequacy decisions by the European Commission, standard data protection clauses, or other legal basis for the transfer of personal information from the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom to the United States.

USER RIGHTS

Depending on your location, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information, such as:

  • The right to access your personal information and receive a copy of it
  • The right to rectify your personal information if it is inaccurate or incomplete
  • The right to erase your personal information or restrict its processing, under certain conditions
  • The right to object to the processing of your personal information, under certain conditions

  • The right to restriction of processing in the following situations:

You can exercise your rights by contacting us at elamri@dris.llc. We will respond to your request within a reasonable time frame, or as required by law. We may ask you to verify your identity before fulfilling your request, to protect your privacy and security.
